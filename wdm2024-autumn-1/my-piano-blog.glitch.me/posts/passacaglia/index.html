Background

The Passacaglia in G minor, originally composed by George Frideric Handel as part of his Harpsichord Suite No. 7, HWV 432, was later famously arranged for violin and viola by the Norwegian composer Johan Halvorsen in 1893. This dramatic and virtuosic arrangement has since become a staple in the string repertoire, but it has also been transcribed for solo piano, showcasing its powerful structure and musical complexity.

A passacaglia is a form built on a repeating bass line or harmonic progression, and in Handel's original composition, the underlying theme creates a foundation over which variations are developed.
