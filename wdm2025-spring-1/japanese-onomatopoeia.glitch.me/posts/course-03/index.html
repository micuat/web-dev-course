擬容語

Movements or actions

    Description
    Giyōgo refers to onomatopoeic words that describe movements, motions, or physical actions, particularly those that involve walking, traveling, or moving from place to place. These words help convey the manner, speed, or nature of a movement, such as how someone is walking, running, or the motion of an object.
    Use
    Giyōgo is particularly effective in describing the rhythm or pattern of movement. They can be used in a variety of contexts, from describing human movement (such as walking, running, or jumping) to natural or mechanical motions (such as the movement of an object or even the flow of water or air).
