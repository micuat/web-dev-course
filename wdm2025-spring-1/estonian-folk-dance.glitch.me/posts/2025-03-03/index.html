Estonian Dance Festival

The Estonian Dance Festival is one of Estonia’s most significant cultural events, held every five years in Tallinn alongside the famous Estonian Song Festival . It is a grand celebration of Estonian folk dance and heritage, bringing together thousands of dancers of all ages to perform traditional and choreographed folk dances in a massive synchronized spectacle.

History

The festival dates back to 1934, when the first Dance Festival was held as a complementary event to the Song Festival. Since then, it has grown into an iconic part of Estonia’s national identity, showcasing the country's deep-rooted love for folk traditions, music, and dance.

The event was especially important during the Soviet era, as it served as a quiet form of cultural resistance, keeping Estonian heritage alive despite foreign rule. Today, it remains a proud symbol of national unity and cultural continuity.

The Festival Today

Held at Kalevi Stadium in Tallinn

  • Features around 10,000 dancers, including children, youth, and adults
  • Performances include both traditional and modern folk dance interpretations
  • Accompanied by live folk music and choirs
  • The festival consists of elaborate formations where dancers, dressed in colorful national costumes, create mesmerizing patterns on the field, telling stories of Estonia’s history, folklore, and nature.
