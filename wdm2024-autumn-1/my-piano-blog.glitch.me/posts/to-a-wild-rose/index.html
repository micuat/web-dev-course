To a wild rose

This piece I actually played in my own high school graduation. It took me about 3 months to fully learn it, from about early February 2022 to late April 2022. I don't recommend learning this piece so early in someones piano playing journey, I would recommend something a bit simpler, but if you really like this piece and strugle with motivation like me - go for it, I know how hard it can be to learn a piece if you don't even like how it sounds. The piece is just stunningly beautiful, works really well as a performance piece and it is a good begginer piece aswell.

Background

"To a Wild Rose" is one of the most well-known and cherished piano pieces composed by Edward MacDowell in 1896. It is the first piece in his "Woodland Sketches, Op. 51", a collection that reflects MacDowellâ€™s deep connection with nature and his love for the American landscape.

The piece is celebrated for its simplicity and lyrical beauty, evoking the image of a delicate wild rose blooming in a tranquil setting. Unlike many complex piano works, "To a Wild Rose" stands out for its minimalist elegance, featuring a gentle, flowing melody that seems to drift naturally, much like the unfolding of a flower. The harmony is soft and understated, allowing the melody to shine and create a sense of quiet reflection.

Due to its serene charm and accessibility, this piece has become a favorite for pianists of all levels and is often performed in both recitals and informal settings. Its timeless appeal lies in its ability to convey deep emotion through a simple yet exquisitely crafted musical narrative.

My rating

Sound - 9.5/10 - Absolutly gorgeous piece.

Difficulty - 4/10 - Quite simple, but has some challenges especially with timing.

Practising - 8/10 - Great piece to practice

Playing/Remebering - 8/10 - Has some repetition, which actually makes the piece harder to remeber and more boring to play. However the piece is extremly beautiful so it doesn't even matter.
