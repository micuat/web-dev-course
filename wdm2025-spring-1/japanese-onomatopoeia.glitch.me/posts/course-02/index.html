擬音語

Sounds made by non-living things and nature

    Description
    Giongo refers to onomatopoeia that imitates inanimate sounds, including mechanical noises, natural phenomena, and other non-living things. It is distinct from Giseigo, which imitates living creatures' sounds.
    Use
    Giongo is widely used for describing noises in the environment or mechanical and natural sounds. It helps bring vivid imagery of how an event sounds, such as the crackling of fire or the ringing of a bell.
Japanese word Romaji Meaning Sound
ゴロゴロ gorogoro Sound of thunder rumbling
シュッ shu Sound of the wind blowing swiftly
バシャバシャ bashabasha Splashing sound, like wading through water
ポタポタ potapota Sound of water dripping
ザーザー zaazaa Sound of heavy rain falling
こぽこぽ kopokopo Water bubbling gently
