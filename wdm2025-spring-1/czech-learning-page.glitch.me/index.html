Lesson 1: Basic Greetings

Learning how to greet others is the first step in becoming comfortable in any language. Czech has both formal and informal ways to say "hello," "goodbye," and other common phrases. These will help you start conversations and make a good impression.

Ahoj - Hello / Hi (informal)
Dobrý den - Good day (formal)
Nashledanou - Goodbye (formal)
Děkuji - Thank you
Prosím - Please / You’re welcome

Example Conversations

Jane: Ahoj! Jak se máš? (Hey! How are you?)

Tomáš: Dobrý den, mám se dobře, děkuji. (Good day, I’m well, thank you.)

Quick Tips

  • "Ahoj" is used among friends, family, and people your own age.
  • "Dobrý den" is more polite and used in professional or formal settings.
  • "Prosím" can be a response to "thank you" or used to politely ask for something.

Lesson 2: Numbers 1 to 10

Being able to count is very useful in real-life situations: ordering food, shopping, asking for directions, or telling time. Let’s learn the numbers from 1 to 10 in Czech!

  • Jedna - One
  • Dva - Two
  • Tři - Three
  • Čtyři - Four
  • Pět - Five
  • Šest - Six
  • Sedm - Seven
  • Osm - Eight
  • Devět - Nine
  • Deset - Ten

Practice Sentences

Kolik je to jablek? (How many apples is that?)

To jsou dvě jablka. (Those are two apples.)

Máme pět knih. (We have five books.)

Fun Fact

The Czech language includes a unique sound “ř” as in the word “tři” (three), which does not exist in many other languages and can be tricky to pronounce. Don’t worry — it gets easier with practice!
