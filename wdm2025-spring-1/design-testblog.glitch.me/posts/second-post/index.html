A little gallery of mine

This is a little collage of a couple things I've drawn

Here I will share some of my recent digital sketches and works I've made. While I'm not entirely happy with many and wish to exclude them or make changes to them, I feel it would be dishonest to do so. Because of that I've included only the most recent and not necessarily my favorite ones of my digital sketches and works exactly as they were left to leave an impression of truthfulness. I feel that this sense of truthfulness creates a more true (silent, albeit important) communication between the viewer and the other party. While my drawings aren't trying to communicate much at all a lot of the time, it's still a window into my experience (or inexperience) and some sense of intent even if they're just silly doodles (which they are). This sense of oddly close connection is scary, because having others see your faults is scary.

Now, I feel that I haven't written enough, so I'll now proceed to drag out this little text bubble a bit before it pops and it's over. I mean, it's not just for the sake of that alone, but also because I think it's fun to write about things you care about deeply. Though my main motivation remains, still, making another paragraph or two, and what other way to fill a paragraph than by talking about filling a paragraph? Pretty neat how you can communicate about communication seemingly quite easily. And while visual communication through art is more direct it can also be more difficult sometimes, especially when tackling more complex ideas such as communication of communication (if we're talking in a more abstract sense).

The smoke "frame" of the image came from my decision to rotate the image late into the process and use the smoke to sort of mask the missing area that emerged from rotating the image and since it was getting quite late, I foresaw that I was done with the drawing.
