Like a Dragon: Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii

Date played: 21.02.2025 - 02.03.2025

quick recap

Our favorite one-eyed yakuza man somehow appears on random island near Hawaii with amnesia. Little boy Noah with his cat Goro saves him from dehydration and possible sunstroke. After that Majima finds himself in a fight (as usual) with random pirates that for some reason still exist in 2024, beats them up, takes their ship, becomes captain and goes on adventure, finding crew members along the way with the aim to show Noah the whole world (or atleast what he can, before returning back to Japan).

aftermath

I've completed game yesterday at 4am and I'am not sure how I feel about all what I've seen. I did enjoyed playing this game, but story was too small for me, only 5 chapters. I understand that this game is more like spin-off about, but it does not help.

Throughout whole game I wasn't sure what to expect, I was prepeared for goofy story, but at the same time part of me was hoping for a heartbraking drama with jokes about man, who tries to understand himself again, which in the end brings some sort of inner conflinct amid Mad Dog and Captain Majima. Turns out, this is in fact a silly game with mediocre Disney-like story. Bad guys are being bad because they are bad, getting punished for being bad. Good guys are being good guys, saving people and beating bad guys. Oh, and Noah is just average Naruto-like kid, I do not care about him at all.

Still, I really enjoyed every second of my playthrough. It really does feel like devs were enjoying making this game too, they included pb everything that was ever related to Majima. Every costume, every karaoke song, almost every main and sub-mission character in Majima's life, all had it appearence in this game. Incredible.

All in all, I can recommend this game even for those, who have no idea about Yakuza series, even though it is better to play it after completing previous games.
