Japanese Onomatopoeia

    What is onomatopoeia?
    Onomatopoeia normally refers to words that represent natural sounds. However, Japanese onomatopoeia is more extensive and might refer to conditions, feelings and actions as well.
    About the website
    This website is aimed at intermediate Japanese learners and designed to divide basic onomatopoeia in Japanese language into 5 categories. The words in the first two groups are followed by the sounds they represent, the ones in the other 3 though are followed by pictures, normally memes, anime or games characters.
    Secret category
    As you can see, there is one more category on the page as well. As a proud One Piece fan, I decided to include the most ridiculous laughers of this anime, which sounds crazy and kinda off-topic, but why not lol.

Categories

  • 擬声語 (Human or animal sounds)
  • 擬音語 (Sounds made by non-living things and nature)
  • 擬容語 (Expresses movements or actions)
  • 擬態語 (Describes conditions or states)
  • 擬情語 (Describes emotions or psychological states)
