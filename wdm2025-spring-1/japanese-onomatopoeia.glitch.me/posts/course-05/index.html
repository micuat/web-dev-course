擬情語

Emotions or psychological states

    Description
    Gijōgo refers to onomatopoeic words that describe emotions, feelings, or states of mind. Unlike other types of onomatopoeia that focus on sounds or appearances, Gijōgo conveys the emotional or mental state of a person or situation, often imitating the intensity or quality of the feeling.
    Use
    Gijōgo words are especially useful in conveying the inner emotional states or mental attitudes of people. These words allow speakers to express complex feelings in a simple, onomatopoeic way and are frequently used in both casual conversation and creative works like manga and novels.
